My Research

I became interested in HCI because I kept noticing a gap — the gap between what AI can do and what it should do. Most systems optimize for efficiency. I'm more interested in what happens to human thinking in the process.

My work spans two connected threads. One is about protection: helping older adults recognize deepfakes, building digital literacy through experience rather than instruction. The other is about learning: understanding how students interact with LLM writing tools in real classrooms, where the scaffolding sometimes helps and sometimes breaks.

Both threads share the same question: when AI takes on cognitive work, does it make us more capable — or less?
